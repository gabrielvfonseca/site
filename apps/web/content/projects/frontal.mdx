Frontal is the company I'm building to close the gap between AI demos and AI systems that real businesses depend on. A prototype that answers a question in a notebook and a system that handles production traffic, real data, and genuine accountability are separated by more than engineering effort. They are separated by a fundamentally different set of constraints.

At Frontal, we build enterprise AI systems. That means every integration we deliver has to be reliable under load, auditable for compliance, and correct enough that downstream decisions, both business and technical, can trust its output. We handle the full stack: data pipelines, model serving, evaluation frameworks, observability, and the application layer that ties them together.

## My role

I'm the founder and CEO. In practice, that means I stay close to everything: product direction, system architecture, and the code itself. Enterprise AI is a systems problem, not a demo problem, and the only way I know to build good systems is to stay hands-on across the stack. I design the architecture, review the critical paths, and write the code that sits at the boundary layers where correctness matters most.

## The approach

Most AI adoption stalls at the prototype because the jump from "it works in a notebook" to "it works in production" is steep and unglamorous. It's about batching, caching, retries, fallbacks, monitoring, versioning, cost tracking, and a dozen other concerns that have nothing to do with model quality and everything to do with operational reality. Frontal is built to absorb that complexity so our clients don't have to.

## Status

Frontal is under active development. I build in public where I can, sharing architecture decisions and lessons learned along the way. If you're working on AI systems at scale, or even just trying to, I'd love to talk.
