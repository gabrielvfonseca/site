I've tried every system. GTD, PARA, Bullet Journal, Notion templates, Obsidian, Roam, Todoist, Things, TickTick, plain text files.

Most lasted two weeks. The one that's lasted three years isn't a system—it's a handful of principles that survive chaos.

## The Core Principle: Protect the Asset

**You are the asset. Not your code, not your company, not your degree.**

If you burn out, everything stops. Every productivity decision flows from this.

- Sleep 7.5 hours minimum. Non-negotiable.
- Morning deep work block: 7-11am. No meetings, no Slack, no email.
- Exercise daily. Walk, run, lift—doesn't matter. Move.
- Real food. Not "meal replacement" shakes.

When I violate these, my output drops 50% within 48 hours. The math is simple: protect the asset, get the output.

## The Daily Rhythm

### 6:30-7:00 — Wake + Light Movement
No phone. Coffee. 10 min stretch/walk. Brain boots up.

### 7:00-11:00 — Deep Work Block
**One** primary task. No context switching.

- Phone in another room (or Forest app)
- Slack/email closed
- One browser tab (or none)
- Noise-canceling headphones + brown noise

What qualifies: writing code, designing architecture, writing docs, solving hard problems.

What doesn't: email, meetings, code review, admin, "quick questions."

### 11:00-12:00 — Shallow Work / Admin
- Email (batch processed)
- Slack (batch processed)
- Code reviews
- Quick admin tasks
- Meeting prep

### 12:00-13:00 — Lunch + Walk
No screens. 30 min minimum outside. This is where solutions appear.

### 13:00-17:00 — Classes / Meetings / Calls
University classes. Team syncs. Customer calls. Investor updates.

I schedule *all* meetings here. Morning is protected.

### 17:00-19:00 — Exercise + Dinner
Gym or run. Real dinner. No work.

### 19:00-21:00 — Light Creative / Learning
- Writing (like this post)
- Reading technical papers/books
- Side project tinkering
- Language study (Portuguese → English)

No pressure. If I'm tired, I read fiction.

### 21:30 — Wind Down
No screens. Kindle or physical book. Lights out by 22:30.

## The Weekly Rhythm

### Sunday: Weekly Review (30 min)
1. **What moved the needle?** (Revenue, product, learning)
2. **What didn't?** (Busywork, distractions, wasted time)
3. **Top 3 for next week:** One must-ship, one must-learn, one must-connect
4. **Calendar audit:** Kill 2 meetings. Move 1 to async.

### Monday: Plan the Week
- Deep work tasks assigned to specific mornings
- Meetings clustered Tue-Thu
- Fri-Sat kept light for overflow/creativity

### Friday: Ship Something Visible
Demo, deploy, write, publish. End the week with proof of progress.

## Task Management: The Anti-System

I don't use a task manager. I use:

### 1. Calendar = Commitment
If it's not on the calendar, it doesn't exist. Deep work blocks, meetings, classes, gym—all on calendar.

### 2. Paper Index Card = Daily Focus
One 3x5 card per day:
```
Front: Top 3 (must do today)
Back:  - Shallow tasks (batch)
       - Ideas/notes (capture)
       - Blockers (need help)
```

Morning: Write top 3. Evening: Cross off. Throw away.

### 3. Obsidian = Knowledge Base
Not tasks. Notes. Architecture decisions. Meeting summaries. Paper highlights. Code snippets.

Structure: `YYYY-MM-DD-topic.md` for daily notes. `topic/` folders for projects. No PARA, no complex tagging. Search works.

### 4. GitHub Issues = Team Work
Personal tasks don't go here. Only collaborative work with clear ownership and acceptance criteria.

## What I Explicitly Don't Do

- **No daily to-do apps** — They optimize for completion, not impact
- **No time tracking** — I know when I'm deep working. The output tells me.
- **No Pomodoro** — Flow > timer. I work in 90-120 min ultradian rhythms.
- **No "second brain" obsession** — My brain + search + paper is enough
- **No productivity porn** — Reading about systems ≠ doing work

## Handling the Chaos

**When university explodes (exams, deadlines):**
- Deep work block shrinks to 2 hours
- Meetings move to async
- Sleep stays 7.5h
- Exercise stays daily
- Everything else drops

**When Frontal explodes (customer fire, investor request):**
- Morning block stays (it's *my* highest leverage time)
- Evening creative time becomes buffer
- Weekend used sparingly, intentionally
- Communicate delays early

**When both explode:**
- Triage: What *must* happen this week?
- Defer: What can wait 2 weeks?
- Delegate: What can someone else do?
- Delete: What doesn't matter?

## The Realization

Productivity isn't about doing more. It's about **doing the right thing consistently.**

My system has 4 rules:
1. **Morning = deep work** (protect it)
2. **Sleep + move + eat** (fuel the asset)
3. **Weekly review** (correct course)
4. **Paper card** (focus the day)

Everything else is noise.

If you want a system, start here:
- Tomorrow: No phone until 11am. One deep work task. 7.5h sleep. 30 min walk.
- Sunday: 30 min review. Write 3 priorities for week.
- Repeat.

After 30 days, you'll have a system. It won't look like mine. It'll look like *you*.

That's the only system that works.
