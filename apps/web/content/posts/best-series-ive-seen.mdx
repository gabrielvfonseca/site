title: "The Best Series I've Seen"
description: "The shows that stayed with me long after the credits rolled. A running list I'll keep updating."
date: 2026-07-14
---

I've forgotten plenty of good shows over the years, but the great ones never really leave. Some changed the way I think, some kept me awake until 4 a.m., and a few made me wish I could watch them again for the first time. This is a list of the series that have stuck with me the most.

**Succession • 2018–2023**  
A family of billionaires constantly destroying each other, yet somehow endlessly entertaining. Every conversation feels like a negotiation, and the writing is unbelievably sharp.

**Mr. Robot • 2015–2019**  
A psychological thriller disguised as a hacker drama. Every season redefines the story, and Rami Malek's performance carries it from beginning to end.

**Game of Thrones • 2011–2019**  
The ending didn't land, but it's hard to overstate what the first six seasons achieved. For years it felt like the entire world stopped every Sunday night to watch together.

**Silicon Valley • 2014–2019**  
The funniest show about startups ever made. Beneath the jokes is an uncomfortably accurate portrayal of founders, investors, pivots, ego, and the absurdity of the tech industry. Somehow it keeps getting more relatable.

**House of the Dragon • 2022–Present**  
A worthy successor to *Game of Thrones*. Slower, more focused, and driven by complex family politics rather than spectacle alone. Every decision feels like it carries generations of consequences.

**The Office (US) • 2005–2013**  
I've lost count of how many times I've rewatched it. Somehow it keeps getting funnier, and the characters end up feeling like people you've actually worked with.

**For All Mankind • 2019–Present**  
One small change to history creates decades of fascinating possibilities. It's optimistic science fiction at its best, blending engineering, politics, and human ambition into something surprisingly emotional.

**WeCrashed • 2022**  
A fascinating look at one of Silicon Valley's most spectacular implosions. Equal parts startup ambition, charisma, and self-delusion.

**Space Force • 2020–2022**  
Not every joke lands, but the cast carries it. A lighthearted workplace comedy that mixes bureaucracy, science, and just enough absurdity.

**The Four Seasons • 2025–Present**  
A warm, witty comedy about long friendships, relationships, and the changes that come with getting older. More heartfelt than it first appears.

**The White Lotus • 2021–Present**  
Every season is a slow-burning social satire wrapped in a luxury vacation. Sharp writing, unforgettable characters, and an uncanny ability to make every interaction feel quietly uncomfortable.
