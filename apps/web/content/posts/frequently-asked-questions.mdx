A running list of the things people ask me over email, DMs, and coffee. It grows as the questions do.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Can't find your question?">
  If something here doesn't cover it, the fastest way to reach me is the [contact page](/contact) or an [anonymous question](/ama).
</Callout>

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Why are you building Frontal?" defaultOpen>
    Enterprise AI is a systems problem, not a demo problem. The distance between
    a prototype that works once and a system that works every time is enormous —
    and that gap is where the real engineering, and the company, lives.
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem title="What does Frontal actually do?">
    We build the systems around AI models that make them dependable at scale:
    correctness, latency, and reliability treated as the product rather than
    features bolted on later.
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem title="What's your stack?">
    TypeScript and Next.js on the front, Rust and Go where latency matters, and
    a lot of boring, well-tested infrastructure in between. I write about the
    details in [my stack](/posts/my-stack).
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem title="Do you take on freelance or advisory work?">
    Rarely, and only when the problem is genuinely interesting. The best way to
    ask is a short, specific message on the [contact page](/contact).
  </AccordionItem>
  <AccordionItem title="Are you hiring?">
    Always looking for people who care about the reliable part, not just the
    clever part. Reach out even if there's no open role listed.
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
