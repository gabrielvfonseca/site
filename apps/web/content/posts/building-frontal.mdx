I'm building a company called [Frontal](https://frontal.dev). We build enterprise AI systems at scale. This is the short version of why.

## The gap I keep seeing

The distance between an AI demo and an AI system is enormous, and almost everyone underestimates it. A prototype has to work once, in good conditions, for a friendly audience. An enterprise system has to work every time — under real load, on messy real data, with someone accountable when it doesn't.

That second thing is hard engineering, and it's where most AI tooling simply stops. The clever part gets all the attention; the reliable part is what actually ships.

## The bet

Frontal is my bet that the durable value in AI isn't the model or the demo — it's the systems around it that make it dependable at scale. Correctness, latency, and reliability aren't features you add later. They're the product.

I'm the founder and CEO, which in practice means I'm in the product, the architecture, and the code all at once. I like it there. The closer I stay to the real problem, the better the thing I build.

## Building in public

I believe in building in public, so I'll keep writing as this develops — what's working, what I got wrong, what I'm learning about turning hard engineering into a company.
